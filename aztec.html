Aztec Exhibit at the Nasworthy Museum

We have somehow acquired the gigantic circular Aztec calendar Sun Stone. Don't ask how. The current centerpiece of our museum collection, the Aztec calendar is a marvel of mathematics and craftsmanship. The Sun Stone was excavated in the 18th century in the middle of the zocalo (town square) in Mexico City, which was built upon the old Aztec capital of Tenochtitlan. We recommend our guests plan to arrive early in the day to line up to see this exhibit. Come early in the season to view this wonder, as we are not sure how much longer we will have it in our collection!
