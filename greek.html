Greek Exhibit at the Nasworthy Museum

We are privileged to have on loan from the Athens and Delphi museums many artifacts from ancient Greece. The golden mask of "Agamemnon", discovered by Heinrich Schliemann in Mycenae is one of our most visited attractions. But don't forget to visit our sculpture room to see the giant bronze statue of Zeus (or is it Poseidon?). These artifacts represent over a thousand years of Greek history - from the time of Homer through the conquest of Alexander, to the Roman occupation. No visit will be complete without experiencing the genius of the Antikytheran Mechanism - a 2,000 year old gear-driven calculator!
